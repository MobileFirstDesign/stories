Peter played with his boat at the Water’s edge. The tall white sail billowed out in the breeze as the little boat bobbed about on the water. Suddenly a big puff of wind caught the sail and carried the little green boat out of Peter’s reach. “Come back, little boat! Come back” he called, but the little boat went bob, bob, bobbing over the tips of the waves and out to sea. Peter was very sad. He ate hardly anything at tea-time and he wouldn’t go along to see the Punch and ]udy show. “Never mind,” said Mummy and Daddy. We’ll get you another boat, just like THE PETER PERKINS.” But Peter knew there would never be another boat quite like it. He lay in bed that night and listened to the waves tossing and tumbling as they chased each other up the beach.
