Splashl Splash! Splashl” went Peter’s hands as they made lovely waves on the water. The little boat was enjoying the fun and bounced merrily over the waves until Peter’s mother said, “Come on, Peter, time for bed.” Peter wanted to take the little boat to bed with him, but Mummy said it wouldn’t be very comfortable sleeping with a boat in the bed. “And you just might roll over on it,” she added, “and break that lovely tall sail. Mummy propped the boat up on the dressing-table and Peter lay in bed and watched his boat until his eyes closed and he fell fast asleep. The following week when he went on holiday to the seaside with his mother and father, the little green boat went, too. There were other little boys on the beach and there were other little boats, but Peter’s boat was the only one with a proper name-just like a real boat.
